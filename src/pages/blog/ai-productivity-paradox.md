New research from Baslandze et al. (NBER, March 2026) surveyed ~750 CFOs and found something surprising: the perceived productivity gains from AI don't always match measured revenue growth.

## The Paradox

Companies investing in AI report feeling more productive. But the numbers tell a more nuanced story — especially when AI is used primarily for cost-cutting versus growth-focused applications.

## What This Means for Education

As AI reshapes job architectures rather than simply eliminating roles, our training and upskilling programs need to evolve just as quickly. The "seniority gradient" emerging in labor markets suggests entry-level pathways are compressing — a critical signal for educators and workforce planners.

**Key insight:** Growth-focused AI use outperforms cost-cutting for actual productivity gains. The implication? Teach people to build with AI, not just replace tasks with it.
